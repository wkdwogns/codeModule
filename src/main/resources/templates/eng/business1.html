    NYPC {}

    NYPC is a coding competition for youths that Nexon has been holding every year since 2016 to raise awareness of programming and offer coding experience through novel and interesting problems.

    Rehabilitation Hospital

    Being the first of its kind in Korea, PURME Foundation NEXON Children's Rehabilitation Hospital was opened in 2016 with major support from Nexon, and the PURME Foundation, along with 10,000 citizens and 500 companies' voluntary donations.

    NEXON Small Library

    Starting with its first one from 2004, NEXON Small Library has been one of the major CSR projects.
    A total of 119 small libraries are currently being employed 112 in Korea and seven overseas including Burundi, Nepal, Cambodia, Laos and over 100,000 books have been donated so far.
    Those libraries are not only used as reading places but also worked as venues for various cultural experiences for around 100,000 children all over the world.

    NEO-JEJU

    Since 2015, Neo-Jeju a youth project for aiming the new Jeju has supported 11,101 people from 92 projects of 29 youth planning teams over the years.
    Starting from 2018, the project has revised its applicant requirements by eliminating regional or age restrictions and more focusing on the theme of Jeju's original culture, its object has been contributing to the discovery of diverse and new cultures in the region.

    Brick Play

    Nexon Foundation partners with non-profit organizations and social ventures to offer qulality play opportunities to children around the world, through the medium of interlocking bricks. Since its launch in 2018, Nexon Foundation's Brick Play Program has donated 16 million bricks to benifit more than 200,000 children across 30 countries, including Korea, USA, Mongolia, Bangladesh and South Africa.
